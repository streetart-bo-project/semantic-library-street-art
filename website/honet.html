Mural of Honet in via del Lavoro 18

Author: Honet
Place: Via del Lavoro, 18
Date: 2012
Current State: Visible
Identifier: streetart:bo:work6
Theme Historical Theme
❯

DESCRIPTION

A large elephant with a castle on its back is being dragged through the air by colourful balloons, three black birds appear ready to poke holes in them. According to the artist, he was inspired for this mural by a huge fountain in the form of an elephant that Napoleon wanted to place in Bastille Square in Paris to celebrate his victories and power. Work stopped after the defeat at Waterloo. The elephant has always been a symbol of strength. In this case, although enormous in size, it is so light that it can be carried by a handful of balloons that can be pierced by a simple bird in flight. The reference to power (even regal power) that can be as great as it is fragile is obvious: Honet nevertheless represents this theme in a delicate and cheerful manner.

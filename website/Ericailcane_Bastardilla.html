Mural of Ericailcane and Bastardilla in Via del Pratello

Author: Ericailcane and Bastardilla
Place: Via del Pratello
Date: 2020
Current State: Visible
Identifier: streetart:bo:work2
Theme Social Theme
Support: Wall and Gate
❯

DESCRIPTION

This large-scale mural is the result of a powerful collaboration between Ericailcane (Italy) and Bastardilla (Colombia), two internationally renowned street artists known for their evocative and symbolically rich styles.
Created in the historic Via del Pratello, a street deeply associated with Bologna’s countercultural and activist spirit, the mural pays tribute to themes of resistance, cooperation, and social struggles.

The composition is divided into two sides. On one side, female figures are shown throwing purple balloons using a surgical mask as a slingshot. On the other side, a group of mice are sheltering behind coins, attempting to protect themselves from the incoming balloons while holding cotton buds. This striking contrast adds layers of meaning related to fragility, and the clash between different social forces.

The imagery throughout the mural speaks of interdependence and shared strength, evoking the struggles of marginalized communities and the importance of solidarity across borders.

The mural was created as part of the CHEAP Street Poster Art Festival, and stands not only as a remarkable piece of public art, but also as a political statement that honors female voices and grassroots movements.
Its presence in a politically active area of Bologna adds further resonance, inviting reflection and dialogue among passersby.

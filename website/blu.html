Mural of Blu in Via Fioravanti 24

Author: Blu
Place: Via Fioravanti, 24
Date: 2013
Current State: Destroyed
Identifier: streetart:bo:work12
Theme City and Social Theme
❯

DESCRIPTION

This large-scale mural by the renowned Italian street artist Blu once adorned the facade of the XM24 social center in Bologna’s Bolognina district. The artwork depicted a vivid and symbolic battle between two opposing forces within the city. On one side, a wealthy and ruthless elite is represented by figures hurling mortadellas—a satirical nod to Bologna's culinary identity turned into a weapon of dominance. On the other side, a grassroots and justice-driven community fights back with pumpkins and watermelons, symbols of resistance and collective identity.

Inspired by the epic clashes in The Lord of the Rings, the mural reflects over fifteen years of social movements, activism, and urban resistance that shaped the identity of XM24. The intricate black-and-white architectural ruins contrast starkly with the vibrant, color-filled zones, creating a dramatic visual narrative that critiques the processes of gentrification and the marginalization of autonomous spaces.

The mural not only served as a political statement but also as a visual chronicle of Bologna’s recent history, representing the values of inclusivity, solidarity, and rebellion that characterized the social center.

In 2016, following a dispute with the municipality regarding the future of XM24 and in protest against the commodification and appropriation of street art, Blu decided to destroy his own mural, painting it over with gray paint. This act was a powerful gesture aimed at preserving the integrity of his work and its message, refusing to let it be co-opted or displayed without context. The destruction of the mural was itself a form of resistance, emphasizing Blu’s commitment to the values behind his art.

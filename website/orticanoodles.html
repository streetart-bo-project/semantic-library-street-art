DESCRIPTION

This mural by the Italian art collective Orticanoodles portrays the face of Irma Bandiera, a young partisan and courier for the Italian Resistance during World War II. She was captured, tortured and killed by fascist forces in 1944, refusing to betray her companions even under brutal interrogation.

Her image is depicted with vibrant, dynamic colors. The use of color and geometric patterns is characteristic of Orticanoodles’ style, blending street art with the tradition of political portraiture.

The mural not only commemorates Irma Bandiera’s courage and sacrifice, but also restores public visibility to a figure often overlooked in historical narratives. By placing her portrait in a public space, the work transforms the wall into a site of memory and resistance, inviting passersby to reflect on the values of freedom, sacrifice, and civil courage.

It is part of a broader movement to reclaim the city through urban art that tells stories of local heroes, giving voice to those who fought for justice and democracy.

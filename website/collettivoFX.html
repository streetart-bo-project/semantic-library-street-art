Mural of Collettivo FX in Dumbo

Author: Collettivo FX
Place: Via Camillo Casarini, 19
Date: 2020
Current State: Visible

DESCRIPTION

This mural by Collettivo FX was created in memory of the Bologna massacre that occurred on August 2, 1980, one of the most tragic terrorist attacks in Italian history. The work represents a woman shedding two enormous tears.

These tears are filled with profound symbolism: in one tear, a clock is frozen at 10:25, the exact time of the bombing at Bologna’s central train station. In the other tear, the bodies of the victims can be seen, paying tribute to the people who lost their lives and the ones who were injured.

The image evokes the unresolved pain tied to the massacre, as well as the slow and complicated path toward truth and accountability. It’s a powerful artistic gesture that blends collective mourning with social critique, reminding viewers of the human cost of terrorism and the importance of remembering.

Located in the regenerated DUMBO district, the mural connects urban renewal with historical memory, demonstrating how public art can serve as a tool for civic awareness and reflection.

DESCRIPTION

This mural by Colombian artist Luis Gutierrez was created to commemorate the 500th anniversary of the Conquest of the Americas. Painted under the portico of Via Zamboni, the heart of Bologna’s university life, the work has become an emblematic presence in the city’s cultural and academic landscape.

Gutierrez’s style is bold, sensual, and sharp, blending expressive drawing with elements of a personal mythology. The mural is a powerful balance between reality and imagination, incorporating references to rituals, archaic myths, and human legends. Characters such as Fidel Castro and Caravaggio coexist in this vibrant narrative space, reflecting a Latin American perspective steeped in symbolism and resistance.

The mural has endured through the years, surviving student protests, political posters, and celebratory graffiti that characterize the vibrant life of Via Zamboni.

In 2014, the mural was restored by a group of students from the Academy of Fine Arts. Their work brought back the original vivid colors, previously dulled by pollution and the passage of time, and reaffirmed the mural’s status as a symbol of memory, resistance, and intercultural dialogue.

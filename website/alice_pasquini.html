Mural of Alice Pasquini in Via del Pratello

Author: Alice Pasquini
Place: Via del Pratello, 69
Date: 2018
Current State: Visible
Identifier: streetart:bo:work7
Theme Identity
❯

DESCRIPTION

The mural by Alice Pasquini, one of Italy’s most prominent female street artists, depicts a young girl holding a bunch of colourful flowers in her hand. Part of her face is hidden behind a curtain, suggesting a sense of introspection. The imagery reflects themes of femininity, vulnerability, and strength that often recur in the artist’s work.

The curtain can also be interpreted as a metaphor for the nature of street art itself—partly revealed, partly hidden, always navigating between exposure and concealment. In her own words, Pasquini notes that “street art is an art that stirs up controversy”. Over the course of her career, she has faced many challenges.

Despite the obstacles, the artist embraces the risk and impermanence of working in public spaces, acknowledging that “it is the risk of signing one’s name”. The mural on Via del Pratello becomes both a personal statement and a poetic reflection on the role of street art in urban environments.

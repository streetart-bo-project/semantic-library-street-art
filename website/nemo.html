Mural of Nemo in Via Orfeo

Author: Nemo
Place: Via Orfeo, 44
Date: 2017
Current State: Visible
Identifier: streetart:bo:work8
Theme Social Theme
❯

DESCRIPTION

The mural created by Nemo in Via Orfeo is a powerful visual commentary on the contradictions of contemporary society. His characters are depicted with a flaccid, grotesque, and soft appearance, almost surreal in their physical deformation. This deliberate aesthetic choice serves as a critique of the modern obsession with perfection and artificial immortality.

Through this work, Nemo denounces the illusion of progress that often blinds us to the underlying decay of humanity. His grotesque figures reflect how, despite technological advancements and social change, humans continue to repeat the same mistakes—caught in a cycle of contradictions, conflict, and forgetfulness that spans millennia.

The mural becomes an uncomfortable mirror, inviting viewers to question ideals of perfection and the societal narratives we unconsciously accept, reminding us of the fragile and imperfect essence of the human condition.

Mural of ANDRECO in Via dello Scalo 32

Author: ANDRECO
Place: Via dello Scalo, 32
Date: 2012
Current State: Visible
Identifier: streetart:bo:work1
Theme Environment
❯

DESCRIPTION

This striking mural by artist ANDRECO represents a large, symbolic tree, inspired by the Philosophical Tree of the 14th-century alchemical tradition.
The composition integrates alchemical imagery and scientific symbolism: at the base of the tree, a crystal and a large egg serve as metaphors for transformation, representing the evolution from organic to inorganic matter, and the balance between nature and human knowledge.

What makes this mural particularly innovative is the use of an eco-sustainable photochemical paint. This special paint actively captures and reduces atmospheric pollutants, particularly nitrogen oxides (NOx), mimicking the function of a real tree by improving local air quality.
The mural is not only a visual tribute to nature and philosophical thought, but also a functional artwork that interacts with the environment, contributing to a cleaner and healthier urban space.

Located in the Bolognina district, the work is part of ANDRECO’s broader artistic practice that blends art, science, activism, and sustainability.
The tree becomes a living diagram of ecological awareness, reminding passersby of the importance of environmental responsibility in urban settings.
